Nvidia Corporation is an American technology company headquartered in Santa Clara, California.

Nvidia was founded in 1993 by Jensen Huang, Chris Malachowsky, and Curtis Priem. It designs and supplies graphics processing units (GPUs), application programming interfaces (APIs) for data science and high-performance computing, as well as system on chip units (SoCs) for mobile computing and automotive markets.

Nvidia is a dominant supplier of AI hardware and software, and its graphics cards are widely used in gaming, professional visualization, data centers, and automotive applications. The company's CUDA platform is a standard for GPU-accelerated computing.

Nvidia's first product was the NV1, a multimedia accelerator released in 1995. In 2011, Nvidia acquired Icera, a fabless semiconductor company, to enter the mobile baseband processor market.
